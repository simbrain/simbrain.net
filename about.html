SIMBRAIN was originally created by Jeff Yoshimi when he was a graduate student in the late 1990's, and later as a postdoc at UC San Diego. He wanted an an easy way to visualize neural networks and so he built Simbrain. Later, as a faculty member in cognitive and information science at UC Merced, Yoshimi began teaching a course using Simbrain, collaborating with students and other faculty, and consulting with interested parties at other institutions. Zach Tosi (now a graduate student in cognitive science at Indiana) became involved in this period. Version 3 of the software is greatly expanded, and will be released in 2015.
